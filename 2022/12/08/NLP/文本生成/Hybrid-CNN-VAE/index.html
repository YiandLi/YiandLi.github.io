A Hybrid Convolutional Variational Autoencoder for Text Generation

本文讨论了 architectural choices 对于 VAE text generation 产生的影响。
在生成文本时，训练VAE模型的核心困难是 KL Vanish，导致 decoder 退化为一个普通的语言模型。

模型核心：是一个由「一维卷积层 one-dimensional convolutional」和「反卷积层 deconvolutional layer」组成的前馈结构。
效果：更快收敛，更好地处理长序列的能力，避免在文本数据上训练VAE模型的一些主要困难（KL vanish）。

VAE

  • A deterministic internal representation zzz (provided by the encoder) of an input xxx is replaced with a posterior distribution q(z∣x)q(z|x)q(z∣x) .
  • Inputs are then reconstructed by sampling zzz from this posterior and passing them through a decoder.
  • to ensure that we can sample from any point of the latent space and still generate valid and diverse outputs, the posterior q(z∣x)q(z|x)q(z∣x) is regularized with its KL divergence from a prior distribution p(z)p(z)p(z) (a Gaussian with zero mean and unit variance).

The total VAE cost is composed of the reconstruction term, i.e., negative log-likelihood of the data, and the KL regularizer:

Jvae =KL(q(z∣x)∥p(z))−Eq(z∣x)[log⁡p(x∣z)]\begin{array}{r} J_{\text {vae }}=K L(q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x}) \| p(\mathbf{z})) \\ -\mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x})}[\log p(\mathbf{x} \mid \mathbf{z})] \end{array} Jvae ​=KL(q(z∣x)∥p(z))−Eq(z∣x)​[logp(x∣z)]​

One can view a VAE as a traditional Autoencoder with some restrictions imposed on the internal representation space.
Specifically, using a sample from the q(z∣x)q(z|x)q(z∣x) to reconstruct the input instead of a deterministic zzz, forces the model to map an input to a region of the space rather than to a single point.
For the model, the most easy way is to prevent this behavior and force the model to find a solution with, on one hand, low reconstruction error and, on the other, predicted posterior distributions close to the prior.
Thus, from every point in the latent space, the decoder could reconstruct a sensible data. And linear operations on different latent variable is available. Whereas this linear operation does not work in the fully deterministic Autoencoder framework.

Deconvolutional Networks

A deconvolutional layer (also called transposed convolutions (Gulrajani et al., 2016)/ fractionally strided convolutions (Radford et al., 2015))/
performs spatial up-sampling of its inputs , and is used in latent variable generative models of images.
Its goal is to perform an “inverse” convolution operation and increase spatial size of the input while decreasing the number of feature maps.

The operation of deconvolutions is :

  1. The global representation is first linearly mapped to another representation with small spatial resolution(分辨率) and large number of feature maps.
  2. A stack of deconvolutional layers is then applied to this representation, each layer progressively increasing spatial resolution and decreasing the amount of feature channels.
  3. The output of the last layer is an image or a text fragment.

其实就可以用 降维encoder(step 1) 和 升维decoder(step 2) 来理解，一个bottleneck 的结构。

Why not use RNN to get output from latent variable?

  1. extremely efficient GPU implementations due to their fully parallel structure
  2. easier to optimize than their recurrent counterparts

Hybrid Convolutional-Recurrent VAE

The whole model is composed of a standard VAE (encoder+decoder) and a recurrent language model

  • VAE - encoder: convolutional layers
  • VAE - decoder: deconvolutional layers
  • RNN: accept 「the activated result of the decoder」 and 「hidden state of last step」

Two recurrent functions are used:

  1. LSTM: capture dependencies between tokens
  2. a stack of masked convolutions ( the ByteNet decoder - Kalchbrenner et al. (2016) )

If without the LSTM, the conditional distribution is:

P(x1,…,xn∣z)=∏iP(xi∣z)P\left(x_1, \ldots, x_n \mid \mathbf{z}\right)=\prod_i P\left(x_i \mid \mathbf{z}\right) P(x1​,…,xn​∣z)=i∏​P(xi​∣z)

If with the LSTM, the conditional distribution is :

P(x1,…,xn∣z)=∏iP(xi∣xi−1,…,x1,z)P\left(x_1, \ldots, x_n \mid \mathbf{z}\right)=\prod_i P\left(x_i \mid x_{i-1}, \ldots, x_1, \mathbf{z}\right) P(x1​,…,xn​∣z)=i∏​P(xi​∣xi−1​,…,x1​,z)

Thus, adding a recurrent layer on top of our fully feed-forward encoder-decoder architecture relieves it from encoding every token into the latent vector and allows it to instead focus on more high-level semantic and stylistic features.

Plus, this model could impose an upper bound on the size of text samples it is able to generate.

Optimization Difficulties

Due to the RNN component added in the decoder, there is also a KLL-Vanish problem.

Bowman et al. (2016)(LSTM-VAE) have proposed to use input dropout and KL term annealing methods to prevent KL Vanish.
These two methods also work for this model.

For KL term annealing, the author have found the following heuristic to work well:

  1. run a model with KL weight fixed to 0 to find the number of iterations it needs to converge.
  2. configure the annealing schedule to start after the unregularized model has converged and last for no less than 20% of that amount.

For input dropout, it may slow down convergence. So here an alternative technique is proposed to encourage the model to compress information into the latent vector: In addition to the reconstruction cost computed on the outputs of the recurrent language model, we also add an auxiliary reconstruction term computed from the activations of the last deconvolutional layer:

Jaux=−Eq(z∣x)[log⁡p(x∣z)]=−Eq(z∣x)[∑tlog⁡p(xt∣z)]\begin{aligned} & J_{a u x}=-\mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x})}[\log p(\mathbf{x} \mid \mathbf{z})] \\ & =-\mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x})}\left[\sum_t \log p\left(x_t \mid \mathbf{z}\right)\right] \end{aligned} ​Jaux​=−Eq(z∣x)​[logp(x∣z)]=−Eq(z∣x)​[t∑​logp(xt​∣z)]​

Since at this layer the model does not have access to previous output elements, it needs to rely only on the zzz vector to produce a meaningful reconstruction.
In this way, the latent vector could be learnt with historyless knowledge and be useful for historyless reconstruction.

The final hybrid reconstruction loss is :

Jhybrid =Jvae +αJaux J_{\text {hybrid }}=J_{\text {vae }}+\alpha J_{\text {aux }} Jhybrid ​=Jvae ​+αJaux ​

α\alphaα is a hyper-parameter.

The final loss for this model is :

Jhybrid =KL(q(z∣x)∥p(z))−Eq(z∣x)[∑tlog⁡p(xt∣z,x<t)]−αEq(z∣x)[∑tlog⁡p(xt∣z)]\begin{array}{r} J_{\text {hybrid }}=K L(q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x}) \| p(\mathbf{z})) -\mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x})}\left[\sum_{t} \log p\left(x_{t} \mid \mathbf{z}, x_{<t}\right)\right] \\ -\alpha \mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z} \mid \mathbf{x})}\left[\sum_{t} \log p\left(x_{t} \mid \mathbf{z}\right)\right] \end{array} Jhybrid ​=KL(q(z∣x)∥p(z))−Eq(z∣x)​[∑t​logp(xt​∣z,x<t​)]−αEq(z∣x)​[∑t​logp(xt​∣z)]​

Also, we could see this function as two VAE that partially share parameters:

  1. one only feedforward
  2. the other combining feed-forward and recurrent parts

The encoder is shared by these two VAEs, so it could be boosted with two targets.
